Source 2 International was a prominent independent semiconductor distributor and electronics broker operating in the heart of Silicon Valley during the personal computing boom of the early 1980s. (1980–1984)

The Business Profile: During that period, the technology sector faced volatile supply chains, swinging between severe chip shortages and sudden excess inventory gluts. Source 2 International capitalized on this environment by operating as a high-volume independent broker.

Location: The company was headquartered at 3920 Freedom Circle, Santa Clara, California, putting it at the epicenter of Silicon Valley's tech manufacturing.

Core Operations: The company operated in the "gray market" or independent distribution tier. It sourced excess electronic components, integrated circuits, microprocessors, and memory chips from major original equipment manufacturers who had over-produced or over-stocked. The exchange used its systems to match buyers and sellers of components, much as the stock exchange matched buyers and sellers of securities. It was a sales channel for equipment makers with unwieldy inventories and a new source of components for manufacturers with parts shortages. 

Founders and Key Leadership:
Robert Solomon, President; Robert founded and built Source 2 International into a powerhouse by capitalizing on the massive, unpredictable fluctuations of the 1980s semiconductor market. Solomon successfully expanded the business from a local Santa Clara brokerage firm into an international trading outfit with extensive North American distribution channels and global trademark protections.

Kirk Jensen, Vice President; Kirk served as principal founder and Vice President. Jensen was a core member of the executive team that managed the company's aggressive trading operations in Santa Clara. Jensen was heavily involved in managing the complex logistics of the semiconductor "gray market" exchange.

Fred Richey, Director of Information Systems; Fred occupied a highly critical operational role at Source 2 International, distinct from the sales teams: Richey was the technologist responsible for designing and maintaining the databases and information systems that tracked global electronic components. The center utilized an IBM compatible Magnuson M80/32 Computer running IBM's OS/VS1 operating system. It hosted an IDMS database and IBM's CICS user interface system, which delivered real-time inventories for the exchange.
